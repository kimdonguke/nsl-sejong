What We Do

Research Areas

Our lab focuses on cutting-edge research in satellite navigation and positioning technologies, bridging theoretical innovation with practical applications.

GNSS Signal Processing

Advanced algorithms for acquisition, tracking, and positioning using GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, and BeiDou signals. Research includes anti-jamming, anti-spoofing, and multipath mitigation techniques.

Learn more →

Integrated Navigation

Sensor fusion of GNSS with IMU, barometers, and vision systems. Development of robust Kalman filter architectures for seamless navigation in GNSS-degraded and denied environments.

Learn more →

Autonomous Navigation

Navigation solutions for autonomous vehicles and UAVs. Combining GNSS/INS with LiDAR, camera, and HD maps for precise localization in urban and challenging environments.

Learn more →

About Our Lab

Navigating the Future

The Navigation System Lab (NSL) at Sejong University's Department of Aerospace Engineering conducts fundamental and applied research in satellite navigation and positioning technologies.

Our interdisciplinary team works at the intersection of signal processing, control systems, and machine learning to develop next-generation navigation solutions for aerospace, automotive, and robotics applications.

  • ✓ State-of-the-art GNSS signal simulator and testing equipment
  • ✓ Collaboration with national and international research institutes
  • ✓ Industry partnerships with leading aerospace companies
  • ✓ Government-funded research projects (NRF, ADD, IITP)
